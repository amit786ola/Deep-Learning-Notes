# 📘 Machine Learning - Regression (Interview Notes)

---

# 🔴 1. Linear Regression Model

## Model Formulation

[
y = X\beta + \epsilon
]

* (y): target
* (X): feature matrix
* (\beta): coefficients
* (\epsilon): error

---

## Closed Form Solution (OLS)

[
\beta = (X^T X)^{-1} X^T y
]

### Key Points:

* Minimizes squared error
* Requires (X^TX) to be invertible
* Not scalable for large datasets

---

## Interview Answer

**Q: Why OLS?**
A: It provides a closed-form optimal solution minimizing squared error but fails with multicollinearity or large datasets.

---

# 🔴 2. Loss Functions

---

## 1. Mean Squared Error (MSE)

[
MSE = \frac{1}{n} \sum (y - \hat{y})^2
]

### Use:

* When large errors are costly
* Clean datasets

### Pros:

* Convex, differentiable

### Cons:

* Sensitive to outliers

---

## 2. Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

[
MAE = \frac{1}{n} \sum |y - \hat{y}|
]

### Use:

* Noisy data
* Outliers present

### Pros:

* Robust to outliers

### Cons:

* Not differentiable

---

## 3. RMSE

[
RMSE = \sqrt{MSE}
]

### Use:

* Business interpretation (same unit as target)

---

## 4. Huber Loss

[
L =
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{2}(y - \hat{y})^2 & \text{if small error} \
\delta(|y - \hat{y}| - \frac{1}{2}\delta) & \text{if large error}
\end{cases}
]

### Use:

* Real-world noisy datasets

---

## 5. Quantile Loss

[
L = \max(\tau(y - \hat{y}), (\tau - 1)(y - \hat{y}))
]

### Use:

* Forecasting
* Prediction intervals

---

## Interview Answer

**Q: When to use MAE vs MSE?**
A: Use MAE for robustness to outliers, MSE when large errors need stronger penalty.

# 🔴 3. Optimization

## Gradient Descent

[
\theta = \theta - \alpha \nabla J(\theta)
]

### Types:

* Batch GD
* SGD
* Mini-batch (best in practice)

# 🔴 4. Assumptions of Linear Regression

---

## 1. Linearity

* Relationship must be linear

**Violation:** Underfitting
**Fix:** Polynomial / tree models

---

## 2. Independence of Errors

* No autocorrelation

**Violation:** Invalid statistical inference
**Fix:** Time-series models, lag features

---

## 3. Homoscedasticity

* Constant variance

**Violation:** Biased confidence intervals
**Fix:** Log transform, weighted regression

---

## 4. No Multicollinearity

* Features not highly correlated

**Violation:** Unstable coefficients
**Fix:** Remove features, Ridge, PCA

---

## 5. Normality of Residuals

* Required for inference

**Violation:** Affects hypothesis testing

---

# 🔴 5. Regularization

---

## Ridge (L2)

[
\min ||y - X\beta||^2 + \lambda ||\beta||^2
]

* Shrinks coefficients
* Handles multicollinearity

---

## Lasso (L1)

[
\min ||y - X\beta||^2 + \lambda ||\beta||_1
]

* Produces sparse solutions
* Feature selection

---

## ElasticNet

[
Loss = MSE + \lambda_1 L1 + \lambda_2 L2
]

---

## Interview Answer

**Q: Why Lasso gives feature selection?**
A: L1 penalty forces some coefficients exactly to zero.

---

# 🔴 6. Evaluation Metrics

---

## R² Score

[
R^2 = 1 - \frac{SS_{res}}{SS_{tot}}
]

---

## Adjusted R²

* Penalizes unnecessary features

---

# 🔴 7. Diagnostics

---

## Residual Analysis

* Residual vs fitted → Linearity
* Q-Q plot → Normality
* ACF plot → Independence

---

## VIF

[
VIF = \frac{1}{1 - R^2}
]

* VIF > 10 → multicollinearity

---

# 🔴 8. Bias-Variance Tradeoff

* Bias → Underfitting
* Variance → Overfitting

Goal: Balance both

---

# 🔴 9. Edge Cases

---

## XᵀX not invertible

* Cause: Multicollinearity
* Fix: Ridge

---

## Large dataset

* Use SGD

---

## Skewed target

* Log transform

---

## Outliers

* Use MAE / Huber

---

# 🔴 10. Advanced Interview Questions

---

### Q: Why squared error?

A: Convex and differentiable → easy optimization

---

### Q: Why regression sensitive to outliers?

A: Squaring magnifies large errors

---

### Q: What is multicollinearity impact?

A: Unstable coefficients

---

### Q: When to use tree models?

A: Non-linear relationships

---

### Q: Can regression be used for classification?

A: Not ideal, use logistic regression
